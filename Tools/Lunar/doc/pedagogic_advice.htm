Teacher Guidance
 

Pedagogical advice: Lunar Lander

  • Students need to work with three different systems during these activities: ToonTalk for modelling and recording data and Excel for plotting data (note there is a tutorial here). Experience from last year has shown that although students will have initial difficulty, they quickly become conversant in using all three systems for the tasks at hand.
  • Students will not have much experience or good understandings of position-time graphs. The idea should be introduced with reference to common position-time graphs such as daily temperature readings. The idea of sampling also needs to be introduced i.e. how frequently data is recorded. An analogy can be made to monthly, daily or hourly temperature readings. Technically the sampling in ToonTalk is a bit tricky - it is "as fast as possible" and is therefore variable depending on the computer and processing load etc. However, you can explain that it is "very fast" e.g. 50 times per second. The important concept to get across is that each data point represents a value at a particular time, and that elapsed time increases as you travel along the position-time graph to the right. You will need to reiterate this point - keep asking students "what does this axis represent?" or similar.
  • Students will have difficulty with the concept of acceleration.  It is a difficult concept. Students will get confused between speed (or velocity) and acceleration. They might say that a moving object is accelerating. They are also likely to say that acceleration is "speeding up" and not recognise it as also slowing down (well, this is confusing anyway - we also use the term deceleration for slowing down, but in a physics sense acceleration means change in speed whether "speeding up" or "slowing down"). You should reiterate that acceleration is a change in speed (velocity).
  • You should explain to students that if they scroll while recording data then they will cause a discontinuity on a position-time graph because the position sensors are relative to the programmer's position (so they should avoid scrolling if possible). You can show an example of this. Also ask students questions about the relationship between the top and bottom of the screen, and what this relates to on a position-time graph.
  • Using F8 to start and stop all robots is an effective way to control the recording of data. E.g. turn off power (F8), set the relevant position, speed, and acceleration sensors/ tools to what you want, give the Export to Excel tool the sensor you want to record, then turn power on (F8) to start recording data and again F8 to stop when finished.
  • You should also instruct students to simply delete (i.e. suck up with Dusty) each Excel tool once it has been used once (and take a new one from the notebook). This is because if more than one Excel tool is running, you will get unpredictable results as both are trying to copy to the clipboard. Also, occasionally kids switch into Excel and then accidentally press "ctrl-c" before pressing "ctrl-v" which has the effect of clearing the clipboard, meaning the data just recorded in ToonTalk will be lost.
