Make my Graph Rules

The rules of Make my Graph

Each player can "challenge" all others to recreate the shape of the graph they have plotted, or "respond" by trying to recreate someone else's graph.

To challenge:

  1. Use the Export to Excel tool from the Lunar Lander Notebook to record some sensor (e.g. up, or up speed) data from a moving Lunar Lander in ToonTalk.
  2. Switch to Excel and paste your data in, then plot your graph.
  3. Make sure to label the axes on your graph (what are you plotting?).
  4. Save your graph as an image file, so that you can upload it to your webreport.
  5. Create your personal game page: go to the Make my Graph Template and click New Report from this template.
  6. Add the graph to your game page (if you don't know how to do that, try this tutorial).
  7. Make sure you say which sensor (e.g. up, or up speed) you plotted, if you forgot to label your graph axes.
  8. Add any hints or tips you think might be necessary.
  9. Publish your page.

To respond:

  1. Create a graph (steps 1-4 above) that is the same shape as the "challenge" you are responding to.
  2. Add this graph as a comment on the challenging game page.
  3. Also explain in the comment how you made the graph.
     
When your challenge is responded to:
If you think the graph that someone posted is the same shape as yours:
say something nice, see if they made the graph the same way you did, and think of another challenge.
If you think the graph that someone posted is not the same shape as yours:
explain what the differences between the two graphs are.

Note

Note that it is the shape of the graph that is most important, rather than the exact number values. You have to use your judgement to say whether you think someone has successfully responded to your challenge.
