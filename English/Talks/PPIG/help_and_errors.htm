Are Visual Agents Good for Help and Error Reporting?

ToonTalk programmers receive on-line help and errors from an animated character called Marty.

There are some advantages over more conventional help and error reporting:

  1. Sustaining the suspension of disbelief. The fantasy that the programmer has entered a virtual world isn't broken.

  2. Social interaction. The information is presented by a virtual social being that users can relate to.

  3. Gestural deixis. One can pick up or point to something and request help from Marty - often an easier way to refer to something than a verbal description.

So why do a majority of ToonTalk programmers dislike Marty?
